European Convention Center Luxembourg

What is ECCL?

The European Convention Center Luxembourg consists of two buildings dedicated to corporate and government events: the main building and the hemicycle. Every year, it hosts more than 400 events, all tailor-made and in an exceptional setting in the heart of Luxembourg's Kirchberg business district. It offers a wide range of services that customers don't have to worry about during their event: Cleaning, security, catering and a wide range of technical equipment. Three times a year, the ECCL hosts the European Council of Ministers, which fully privatizes the premises and incorporates its own security elements.

My work

As Event Assistant, I was in charge of supporting the work of Grégory, Event Manager at ECCL for almost 10 years. We took over from our sales colleagues as soon as the rental contract was signed by the customer. I was in charge of coordinating the various players involved in making a good event: technical, security, cleaning, catering, the direct customer and, very often, external service providers. I was also close to the customers, guiding them in their choice of services within the ECCL. I also worked on the event follow-up part, for which we produced the annual activity report with the rest of the team.

What did i learned ?

During this 6-month period, I discovered the world of business events. A facet of the events that I had never explored before. I learned to perfect my personal service and to become more demanding with myself to deliver quality work that meets customer expectations. It's an exercise that has given me a lot in terms of self-perception. Managing a 3,500 m² building and coordinating 5 to 6 different departments requires clarity and rigor in one's work in order to distribute information correctly.

Alive Group

What is Alive?

Alive is France's 3rd leading event equipment rental company. With a nationwide presence, Alive offers an exceptional catalog of items as varied as its clientele. From the smallest corporate conferences to the biggest festivals, it's hard to miss when it comes to combining quality and quantity.

My work

At Alive, I've held two positions that have both taught me a lot. First, I was a stock manager in the lighting section of the equipment pool. I was in charge of equipment maintenance and organizing the departure and return of equipment. I then worked as a sales representative for Alive's broadcast division, which specializes in rental of equipment for filming. I was in close contact with Alive's "small" customers to offer them a tailor-made rental service that met their expectations.

What did i learned ?

In both positions, priority has always been given to the quality of service and equipment offered to customers. I've rubbed shoulders with a lot of industry professionals, both internal and external. It was at Alive that I most developed my adaptability and organizational skills. These are essential qualities in an environment where everything has to be redone for each new project.

Brevet de Technicien Supérieur Audiovisuel: option métiers du son

What is this BTS?

This diploma is a pathway to specialization in audiovisual fields. It opens doors to a wide range of career possibilities, and helps you understand the close links between the 4 main specializations: sound, image, video and post-production.

What did i learned?

In the sound option, I discovered how to professionally produce a sound production, from its raw capture to its final mix. The main technical objective was to make the audio content as pure as possible, so as to offer an exceptional final rendering to the editor in charge of video assembly. We were also in charge of various audio manipulations on software in dedicated mixing rooms.

What i wanted to do after?

After this unique experience, I was divided between audiovisual and many other fields such as philosophy or foreign languages. Despite this, I continued to work as a freelancer, which was booming after the BTS.

Freelance

Why freelance?

The question of freelance status came up when I had to find a company to take me on as part of my audiovisual training, which began in September 2017. On my father's advice, I decided to take the plunge and complete my training course with direct field experience.

My work as a freelance

Although I didn't always focus on sound, which was my chosen field of study, my freelance work offered me a wide range of assignments. Why did i stoped ?

Baccalauréat Technologique: Science Technologique de l'Industrie et du Développement Durable (STI2D)

What is this BAC?

This technological BAC is purely oriented towards sustainable development and its link with industrial professions. When I took this exam, there were 4 options: digital, eco-design, energy and architecture. It's a BAC that opens the door to many different sectors, thanks to its versatility and modernity. At my school, the classrooms were very well equipped for this kind of practice. For example, a 3D printer was available right in our classroom for designing our projects.

What did i learned?

I chose the ITEC option (Technological Innovation and Eco-design) in which I learned how to carry out a project by starting with an idea, then drawing up specifications, conceptualizing the project using 3D software and finally materializing the project using 3D printing. I learned how to defend a project in front of a panel of judges and highlight its strengths and advantages.

What i wanted to do after?

After these studies, which I really enjoyed and learned a lot from, I decided to change direction and go into a sector that intrigued me: audiovisual. My father had worked in the events industry for a long time, so he was able to guide me in my choice of direction. I was convinced that I could only succeed in this field if I put all my passion and ambition into it.

CAM

I discovered computer-assisted music at the age of 17, two years before starting my BTS in sound. I come from a Polish family for whom music is part of every meal. Singing, violins, accordion and double bass rocked my entire youth, and I owe them a great deal for instilling in me what is for me the primary interest of music: creating memories. I didn't play any instruments on a regular basis, despite this and two years of music theory, followed by the purchase of a piano at the age of 12 and a ukulele much later, which I still carry with me when I move house. CAM, however, gave me the beginnings of my desire to build with my hands on digital technology. With my sound software, I have the impression that I can reproduce any sound and arrange them in my own way, without having to be a conductor or know how to play 5 different instruments. Composing is an escape that instantly reconnects me with my musical identity.
